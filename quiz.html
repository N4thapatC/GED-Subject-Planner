Which of the following factors contributed significantly to the outbreak of the American Civil War?

The American Civil War, fought from 1861 to 1865, was a pivotal conflict in United States history. It was primarily a battle over the future of slavery, with the Northern states (Union) fighting to preserve the nation and end slavery, while the Southern states (Confederacy) sought to form a separate nation to maintain their way of life, which relied heavily on enslaved labor. The war concluded with the Union’s victory, leading to the abolition of slavery and significant changes in the social and political structure of the United States.

Chief Justice Earl Warren wrote that ‘separate educational facilities are inherently unequal,’ striking down the precedent set by Plessy v. Ferguson (1896). This landmark ruling emphasized that segregation deprived African American students of equal educational opportunities, violating the Equal Protection Clause of the Fourteenth Amendment.”
“In Brown v. Board of Education (1954), the Supreme Court recognized the psychological effects of segregation on African American children. Evidence presented during the case, such as studies by psychologists Kenneth and Mamie Clark, demonstrated that segregation instilled feelings of inferiority among black students. The Court concluded that such feelings harmed their development, reinforcing the argument that ‘separate but equal’ facilities were inherently unequal.”
ข้อ 7/40 Social Studies

Which sentence from the passage shows the author’s opinion rather than a neutral fact?

The framers of the U.S. Constitution designed the government so no single branch could dominate the others. The legislative, executive, and judicial branches each have distinct powers, but they also have ways to limit each other.

For example, in the early 1950s, President Harry Truman ordered the federal government to seize control of steel mills to prevent a strike during the Korean War. Truman believed the strike would harm the war effort. However, the Supreme Court ruled in Youngstown Sheet & Tube Co. v. Sawyer (1952) that the president had exceeded his constitutional authority. The Court’s decision limited presidential power, reinforcing that the executive branch cannot act without congressional approval in such situations.

Some praised the Court’s ruling as a vital defense of liberty against an overreaching executive. Others criticized the decision as shortsighted, arguing it weakened the president’s ability to respond quickly in times of crisis. The case became a lasting example of how separation of powers functions in practice.

What is the difference in kinetic energy between the two kicks?

Objects in motion have kinetic energy (KE), as given by this formula:

\( KE = \frac{1}{2} m v^2 \)

where m is the mass of the object (in kg) and v is the velocity (in m/s). A 3 kg soccer ball is kicked twice. It is kicked at 4 m/s on the first kick and 7 m/s on the second kick.

ข้อ 27/40 Science

Which position regarding giant ground sloth extinction does this evidence best support?

The Disappearance of the Giant Ground Sloth

The giant ground sloth, an extinct mammal once found across the Americas, vanished thousands of years ago. Scientists continue to debate the main cause of its disappearance, and two competing explanations are most often discussed.

One group of researchers argues that early humans were responsible. When humans migrated into the Americas, they hunted many large animals for food and resources. Archaeological evidence shows bones of giant ground sloths with cut marks, suggesting they were butchered. Supporters of this theory believe that the sloths, which reproduced slowly, could not withstand the intense hunting pressure.

Another group of scientists believes that climate change played the larger role. As the last Ice Age ended, global temperatures rose. Grasslands where the giant sloths thrived began shrinking, replaced by wetlands and forests. Fossil evidence indicates that sloth populations declined sharply in areas where vegetation shifted, even in places with little sign of human presence.

Recent pollen samples from ancient lake beds show that grasslands disappeared quickly in many regions about 10,000 years ago. The loss of this food source would have made survival difficult for the sloths.

ข้อ 29/40 Science

What additional evidence should the second group of scientists collect to strengthen their hypothesis?

The Andes Mountains and Ancient Farming

1. The Andes Mountains in South America are home to high-altitude valleys where ancient civilizations relied heavily on rainfall and glacial melt for farming. Because farmland was scarce, communities developed terraces and irrigation channels to capture and conserve water. Without this system, crops like potatoes and quinoa would not have thrived at such elevations.

2. Historical records and archaeological evidence indicate that around 1,200 years ago, a period of prolonged drought may have contributed to the decline of several Andean settlements. Food shortages increased, and some populations moved to lower valleys. Scientists are investigating whether climate change was a major factor in this social disruption.

3. One group of scientists analyzed lake sediments from Andean basins. They found layers showing reduced concentrations of aquatic plant pollen during drought years, along with an increase in microscopic charcoal, suggesting that dry conditions led to more frequent natural fires.

4. A second research group studied the thickness of sediment layers deposited by rivers flowing through the valleys. They hypothesized that reduced rainfall led to a drop in vegetative cover, causing increased erosion. This process would deposit larger amounts of soil in lake basins, creating a clear signal in the sediment record during dry periods.
ข้อ 30/40 Science

What is the mechanical advantage of the jack?

Mechanical advantage (MA) measures how much a machine multiplies the input force to produce an output force.

Formula : \( MA = \frac{\text{Output force}}{\text{Input force}} \)

A screw jack lifts a platform with an output force of 5,000 N. The worker turns the jack applying an input force of 400 N.
